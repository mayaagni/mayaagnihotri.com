My Story

I've never been good at just one thing. For a long time, I thought that was the problem.

So many people around me seemed to have it figured out — one major, one career path, one clear direction. My path has always been all over the place. I studied CS and business at UNC Chapel Hill, interned at AT&T and Nike, lived in five countries, founded a tech fraternity, and played club basketball — all while trying to figure out what I actually wanted to build.

The problems I kept coming back to were always personal — managing my ADHD, clearing my skin after a decade of failed treatments, navigating dating in New York, figuring out why the tools that are supposed to help us live better so often don't. They all felt solvable. But I couldn't find a clear path to solving them — so I decided to start building one myself.

That's what led to option.bloom — my personal brand and platform where I document, build, and share everything I'm working on. The name comes from the article Option Rot — the idea that if you don't commit to one path, your options start decaying. I decided to flip that. Instead of letting my options rot, I'd make them bloom — pursuing everything, exploring every idea, and trusting that the through-line would reveal itself.

And it did. Data and AI are incredibly powerful, but unless you're deep in the tech world, there's no clear path to using them to actually better your life. That's what I aim to solve. I started with Debrief Dating — a feedback app for daters, because people don't fail at dating because of bad algorithms. They fail because they don't understand themselves. And that's just the start.

By day, I'm a software engineer at Nike building dashboards and data pipelines. I'm also pursuing my M.S. in CS at Georgia Tech with a focus on real-world AI applications. My goal isn't just to become a powerful engineer — it's to connect the right tools with the right people so they can spend more time doing what they love.
